Biryani

Description

Biryani is a fragrant, layered rice dish from South Asia combining aromatic basmati rice with spiced meat (chicken, lamb, or goat) or vegetables. It's known for its complex flavors from spices like saffron, cardamom, and cloves, and its distinctive cooking method where partially cooked rice and meat are layered and slow-cooked together.

Ingredients (serves 4)

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 17.9 ounces chicken (cut into pieces)
  • 0.5 cups yogurt
  • 2 pieces onions (thinly sliced)
  • 2 pieces tomatoes (chopped)
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 3 pieces green chilies (slit)
  • 0.3 cups mint leaves
  • 0.3 cups cilantro leaves
  • 2 tablespoons biryani masala powder
  • 0.5 teaspoons turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoons red chili powder
  • 2 pieces bay leaves
  • 1 pieces cinnamon stick
  • 4 pieces cardamom pods
  • 4 pieces cloves
  • 1 pinch saffron strands
  • 3 tablespoons warm milk
  • 4 tablespoons ghee or oil
  • salt to taste

Steps

  1. Soak rice and saffron: Wash and soak 2 cups basmati rice in water for 30 minutes. In a small bowl, soak 1 pinch saffron strands in 3 tablespoons warm milk.
  2. Marinate chicken: Mix 17.9 ounces chicken (cut into pieces) with 0.5 cups yogurt, half of 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste, 0.5 teaspoons turmeric powder, 1 teaspoons red chili powder, half of 0.3 cups mint leaves and 0.3 cups cilantro leaves, and salt. Marinate for at least 30 minutes.
  3. Fry onions: Heat 4 tablespoons ghee or oil in a heavy-bottomed pot. Fry 2 pieces onions (thinly sliced) until golden brown and crispy. Remove half for garnish and set aside.
  4. Cook chicken masala: In the same pot with remaining onions, add 2 pieces bay leaves, 1 pieces cinnamon stick, 4 pieces cardamom pods, and 4 pieces cloves. Add remaining 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste and saut√© for 15 minutes. Add 2 pieces tomatoes (chopped) and cook until soft. Add marinated chicken and 2 tablespoons biryani masala powder. Cook until chicken is 80% done.
  5. Parboil rice: In a large pot, bring water to boil with salt and whole spices. Add drained rice and cook until 70% done (rice should still have a bite). Drain immediately.
  6. Layer the biryani: Layer half of the parboiled rice over the chicken. Sprinkle with remaining 0.3 cups mint leaves, 0.3 cups cilantro leaves, 3 pieces green chilies (slit), fried onions, and saffron milk. Add remaining rice on top. Drizzle with ghee.
  7. Dum cooking (steam): Cover the pot tightly with aluminum foil, then the lid. Cook on high heat for 33 minutes , then reduce to lowest heat and cook for 25-30 minutes. Turn off heat and let it rest for 5 minutes without opening.

    8. Serve: Gently mix the biryani with a fork from bottom to top. Serve hot with raita
