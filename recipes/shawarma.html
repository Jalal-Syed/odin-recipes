Shawarma

Description

Shawarma is a popular Middle Eastern street food featuring thinly sliced, marinated meat (chicken, beef, or lamb) traditionally cooked on a vertical rotisserie. The spiced meat is wrapped in warm pita or flatbread with fresh vegetables, pickles, and creamy garlic sauce or tahini. Known for its bold flavors from spices like cumin, paprika, and turmeric.

Ingredients

  • 21.4 ounces chicken thighs (boneless, thinly sliced)
  • 0.5 cups plain yogurt
  • 3 tablespoons lemon juice
  • 4 pieces garlic cloves (minced)
  • 3 tablespoons olive oil
  • 2 teaspoons cumin powder
  • 2 teaspoons paprika
  • 1 teaspoons turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoons coriander powder
  • 0.5 teaspoons cinnamon powder
  • 0.5 teaspoons cayenne pepper
  • 1 teaspoons salt
  • 0.5 teaspoons black pepper
  • 4 pieces pita bread or flatbread
  • 2 pieces tomatoes (sliced)
  • 1 pieces cucumbers (sliced)
  • 2 cups lettuce (shredded)
  • 0.5 pieces red onion (thinly sliced)
  • 0.5 cups pickles
  • 6 pieces garlic (for sauce)
  • 0.5 cups mayonnaise or neutral oil
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice (for sauce)
  • 0.3 cups tahini sauce (optional)

Steps

  1. Marinate the chicken: In a large bowl, mix 0.5 cups plain yogurt, 3 tablespoons lemon juice, 4 pieces garlic cloves (minced), 3 tablespoons olive oil, 2 teaspoons cumin powder, 2 teaspoons paprika, 1 teaspoons turmeric powder, 1 teaspoons coriander powder, 0.5 teaspoons cinnamon powder, 0.5 teaspoons cayenne pepper, 1 teaspoons salt, and 0.5 teaspoons black pepper. Add 21.4 ounces chicken thighs (boneless, thinly sliced) and coat well. Cover and refrigerate for at least 120 minutes , preferably overnight.
  2. Make garlic sauce (toum): In a food processor, blend 6 pieces garlic (for sauce) until smooth. Slowly drizzle in 0.5 cups mayonnaise or neutral oil while blending until thick and creamy. Add 2 tablespoons lemon juice (for sauce) and blend. Refrigerate until serving.
  3. Cook the chicken: Heat a large skillet or grill pan over medium-high heat. Add marinated chicken in a single layer and cook for 14 minutes per side until charred and cooked through. You can also broil in the oven at 450Â°F for 15-20 minutes, flipping halfway.
  4. Rest the meat: Remove chicken from heat and let it rest for 5 minutes . Then slice or chop into smaller pieces.
  5. Warm the pita: Heat 4 pieces pita bread or flatbread in a dry pan for 1 minute per side or wrap in foil and warm in the oven.
  6. Assemble shawarma: Spread garlic sauce and 0.3 cups tahini sauce (optional) (if using) on warm pita. Add 21.4 ounces chicken thighs (boneless, thinly sliced), 2 pieces tomatoes (sliced), 1 pieces cucumbers (sliced), 2 cups lettuce (shredded), 0.5 pieces red onion (thinly sliced), and 0.5 cups pickles. Drizzle with more sauce.
  7. Wrap and serve: Fold the pita tightly around the filling. Serve immediately with extra sauce on the side. Enjoy hot!

For best results, marinate overnight. You can use beef or lamb instead of chicken. Traditional shawarma uses a vertical rotisserie, but this stovetop/oven method works great at home. Toum (garlic sauce) is essential - don't skip it!
